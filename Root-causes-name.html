Root Cause #1

Investment into oil is cheaper than clean energy because the infrastructure for oil usage has already been developed on a
global scale, and investment into clean energy is expensive.

Evidence:

Oil has been the dominant energy source since the 29th century.

Explanation:

Oil is a cheaper and less advanced energy source than Solar, and thus was discovered earlier.

Evidence:

The cost of solar infrastructure has decreased drastically over the past decade.

Explanation:

This is because further investment makes products cheaper over time.

Root Cause #2

Climate change negatively impacts the economy because the economy is dependent on a certain standard of human health, climate, infrastructure, and water-bound resources, all of which are affected by climate change.

Evidence:

Climate change negatively affects agricultural output, human health, and water resources.

Explanation:

The economy is dependent on these factors, so we can extrapolate that climate change affects the economy as a consequence of these impacts.

Evidence:

Studies show that high temperatures are not optimal for developing economies and economic production.

Explanation:

Climate change raises global temperatures, so it is implied that climate change harms economies as a consequence of higher temperatures.
